# Warmline — Slide Deck (6 slides)

> 3-minute stage pitch + live demo. One idea per slide, big type, minimal text.
> Speaker notes are the spoken beats; the bullets are what's on the screen.

---

## Slide 1 — Hook

**The best hire, job, or investor you ever landed came through someone you knew.**

- Warm intros convert up to **10x** better than cold outreach.
- Yet there's **no marketplace** for them.

> Speaker: "Think of the best job, hire, or investor you ever landed — I'd bet it came
> through someone you knew. Warm intros convert up to 10x better than cold outreach. And
> yet, there is no marketplace for them. We built one."

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem: one-sided & inferred

**LinkedIn, Happenstance, The Swarm all work one way:**
*you* search → the software *guesses* who in your network might help.

- They scrape the other person's data to infer a fit.
- **The other person never raised their hand.** They don't even know they're in the system.

> Speaker: "Every incumbent is one-sided and inferred. You search, software guesses, and
> the person on the other end never opted in. That's the gap."

---

## Slide 3 — Our Flip: two-sided & declared

**Everyone declares two things:**

- **Offer** — who they are / what they can help with.
- **Ask** — what they need right now.

Post an ask → we match it **semantically** against declared offers across your **warm**
network (friends + friends-of-friends) → and show you the **mutual who connects you**.

> Speaker: "We flip it. Both sides declare an offer and an ask. Post an ask, and we search
> your warm network — friends and friends-of-friends — for whoever's *declared* offer matches,
> semantically. Both people already raised their hand."

---

## Slide 4 — Live Demo (the money slide)

**Ask:** *"a technical cofounder who knows payments infrastructure"*

1. Ranked **warm matches** appear — **Samuel Okoro**, ex-Adyen, #1.
2. Zero shared keywords with *"billing and ledger systems at Adyen"* — **semantic** match.
3. The graph animates the warm path: **You → Priya → Samuel**.
4. One click → **Claude drafts the intro in Priya's voice**, double-opt-in.

> Speaker (drive the product): "No keyword overlap — this is semantic. And instead of
> dropping a stranger on you, we show the mutual who connects you, then write the intro
> *they'd* send. One click."

---

## Slide 5 — Business & GTM

**Where the graph comes from:** import connections to map who-knows-who —
but offer/ask are **always declared, never inferred.**

**Who pays:**

- Recruiters, founders raising, sales & BD — anyone who lives and dies by warm intros.

**Why it compounds:** every person who joins and declares makes every match denser.

> Speaker: "You import connections for the graph topology only — the intent is always
> declared. Recruiters, founders, BD teams pay for this. And it's a network effect: each
> new declaration makes the whole marketplace more valuable."

- Next: **mobile + push** — *"someone in your network can help with your ask."*
- That's the whitespace. That's what we built.

> Speaker: "A declared, two-sided layer over a warm second-degree graph — that's the

## Backup — likely judge Q&A (keep off-slide, in your pocket)

- **Isn't this LinkedIn?** No — LinkedIn is inferred and one-sided. We use connections only
  for graph *topology*; offer/ask are always declared.
- **Cold start?** The declared layer compounds within communities; seed by importing edges,
  then each declaration makes matches denser. (The demo shows a populated world.)
- **Why warm vs cold?** Conversion. A mutual's intro is the highest-trust channel there is.
- **What's the AI doing?** Two things: semantic matching (ask ↔ offer) and drafting the intro
  in the mutual's voice. The matching needs no shared keywords; the draft is one click.
